Andreas Antonopoulos appears in the Early Days of Ethereum corpus mainly as an important figure in the surrounding Bitcoin world rather than as an Ethereum contributor.

In Episode 14, Anthony Di Iorio references Andreas's observation that almost nobody understands Bitcoin instantly, using it as a foil for his own claim that he "got it right away." In [CoinTalk 015](/videos/cointalk-015-btcmiami-debate/), the moderator cites one of Andreas's standard lines about Bitcoin being the "first app" of blockchain technology while setting up the Ethereum versus [Mastercoin](https://www.omnilayer.org/) versus [BitShares](https://bitshares.org/) discussion. [Amir Taaki's Episode 9](/videos/episode009-amir-taaki/) and the [Bitcoin Expo 2014](/articles/bitcoin-expo/) page also place Andreas among the notable speakers and personalities circulating around the same conference scene in which Ethereum was first being pitched.

That is the pattern the repo supports: Andreas was one of the best-known educators and interpreters in the room when Ethereum emerged, and his language helped frame the wider conversation even though he was not part of Ethereum itself.
